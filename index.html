SpendHQ is an all-in-one AI-powered spend intelligence dashboard web app for Fortune 500 companies. It helps them see where their money is going by connecting all of their suppliers and relevant third-party software.

Problem

SpendHQ had no professional designer before I joined the team, resulting in a dated, inconsistent, and clunky experience and interface. It was my job to lead and redesign the entire user experience and interface to modern design standards.

Solution

Combining data from competitive research, user interviews, and testing with clients including Pepsi and Under Armour, I analyzed user analytics collected through HotJar and Pendo (NPS, stickiness, time on site, retention, and heat mapping). I built personas including users who need simple reporting to CEOs who need detailed data refining and filtering. I informed a new minimized, cohesive, and modern experience and interface from scratch.

The product owner and I reviewed the analytics every few weeks to verify the new experiences and design enhancements were performing as we released them iteratively. This allowed us to refine each update and slowly bring the users up to date, rather than shocking them with a new experience and interface all at once.

Wick

100% User Approval Rating of the New Well-being Science Education & Tracking Experience

Role: Mid-level UX Researcher, Senior UX/UI Designer, and Consultant UI Developer

Tools: Adobe XD, Brand Design, Card Sorting, Moodboarding, Pesrsona Development, User Interviews, User Testing, and Whiteboarding

Hi-fi Prototype Hi-fi Screenshot Lo-fi Screenshot Moodboard Style Guide User Interview Results User Testing Script Map Whiteboard

Wick is a mobile app that makes the journey of improving one’s well-being a comforting and enjoyable adventure.

Problem

Learning and applying the science of well-being is a time-consuming, difficult, and confusing endeavor. It requires learning a large amount of sometimes complicated subject matter and tracking several goals on one’s journey to improved well-being.

Solution

We created a gamified, step-by-step well-being improvement journey and education mobile app. This app breaks down the science into bite-sized, easy-to-understand language and gives weighted rewards for each step taken to learn and apply the science to daily life.

Research included a SWOT analysis of the top 25 competing apps in the health and wellness space (existing user experiences, features, flows, visual language, and UI patterns), persona creation (adults 19-40 at all different stages of a well-being journey), and phone interviews collecting quantitative and qualitative data (current well-being apps used, duration, device used, likes and dislikes of their experiences), their current well-being improvement behaviors and their duration and importance to them, and their overall interest in Wick’s mission.

We designed the experience and interface from the ground up with this data, starting with card sorting, then whiteboarding, brand and design system development, lo-fi prototypes, and a hi-fi prototype, adjusted based on feedback from testing our lo-fi prototype.

We verified acceptance and satisfaction via feedback during user testing of our lo-fi prototype, then performed a second user test with a hi-fi prototype evolved upon findings of the lo-fi test. We utilized our research group to compare results to an established user base.

All users agreed that they enjoyed the journey format of the app and that they preferred our experience over the other apps they were currently using on their well-being journey.
