Case Study

Wick

At OddOwls LLC, Wick was all about making achieving your well-being goals easy and fun, which brought us to gamification. It encapsulated not only our desire to create something that will actually help people, but also highlighted our focus on it being a shared journey.

We landed on the tagline “An adventure to a better you,” and a mission statement of “To make the journey of self-improvement fun, simple, and accessible.”

Research

We conducted some competitive research on the top 25 competing apps in the Health and Wellness space, including a SWOT analysis that analyzed the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats from their existing user experiences, features, flows, visual language and UI patterns.

We found that a majority of them employed a similar visual language, they primarily used a clean, minimal aesthetic. We also found a lack of robust gamification which excited us about providing for that niche.

We created personas based on who our ideal user would be. After narrowing down to 3 different persona types, including our core target group of adults aged between 19-40 years old, and at different stages of their well-being journeys, we reached out to them for phone interviews.

We collected quantitative and qualitative data ranging from device type, hourly usage, number of existing or previously used health apps, to which self help/health behaviors they engage in and for how long, and gauged interest in such an app.

We asked if they would ever consider using an application like ours, what they thought their most important positive habits were, and more. We found that they had an overall lack of interest in repetitive and “boring” interactions with the applications they have been using.

UX Design

There were many ideas for names, but we settled on Wick. We imagined our app being the torch that accompanied you on the path to self-improvement. Wick was a short and catchy name that immediately brought the image of fire into our minds. We chose a font that had an earthy, grounded, rugged feel.

When thinking about what our brand should be, we kept coming back to the words, “Journey,” and, “Adventure.” Most of us had played the game “Firewatch”, and were inspired by the warm and exciting feelings of adventure, connection to and immersion in nature. Cool greens and blues were selected in contrast to the yellow and orange CTAs to communicate this.

The language was down to earth, comforting and empathetic. The sans-serif was modern enough to not look out of place in a self-help app, but still resembled the fonts used in state parks and hiking trails. We knew we had to have some form of a fire motif in our logo. We settled on having the flame rest in the crossed arms of the W, like a fire brazier.

Once we established our relative user flows, we created moved onto wire-framing, and then to a lo-fi mockups and a lo-fi interactive prototype for testing. We came up with a Design System for every UI component within the app.

UI Design

Part of our visual language was having beautifully illustrated visuals throughout the entire app and using iconography related to camping and adventure. We encouraging repeat app uses through getting points for completing tasks (with weighted rewards based on the difficulty of the task) and earning badges. Which melded well with the friendly writing tone and positive encouragement throughout the process of use.

Next was mocking up the igh-resolution designh for each screen in the user flow. We fleshed out every possible use state identified in our lo-fi prototype and gave visual life to it using our established visual language. Using Adobe XD, we created an interactive hi-fi prototype for use in final testing before development.
